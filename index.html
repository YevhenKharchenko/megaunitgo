The Room Listens.
The Room Learns

We had one stupid question: What happens when the walls pay attention?
Now the Unit lives at Plange Mühle 1 — a space where your silence, your movement, even your sweat feed the system. It doesn’t try to trick you. It reacts. People walk out realizing they weren’t solving puzzles; they were being measured.

Forget polished wood and fake padlocks. This thing began as a mess of sensors in a basement north of the Rhine.

We Never Meant to Build This.

It started as temperature-mapping research.
Then someone wired an old heart-rate monitor.
Someone else deleted the “solution” file.

By the third test, the Unit was keeping a door shut until the person inside calmed down.

It isn't "AI." It's just sensitive — and slightly resentful.

Glitches are not bugs; they're feedback.

Hardware Held Together with Spite and Zip Ties

  • Metal sheets that hum when you press too hard

  • Lights that surge when anxiety spikes

  • A fan that speeds up with group volume

  • Whole system running on recycled server boards

We bought half of it off eBay, the rest from scrap heaps behind factories. You can tell.
